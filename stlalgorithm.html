.stl Algorithm


This algorithm is used to model a 3D spring using the equation of a helix to calculate the various points on the spring. Once the points are known, an triangulated surface is generated whihc is defined by a unit normal and vertices using a three dimensional Cartersian coordinate system. These triangles are respresented in a specific format (.stl) which can then be used for rapid prototying, 3D printing and computer-aided manufacturing. The flow of the algorithm is as follows :


The equation of the helix in Cartesian coordinate system is given as :
    x = [1 - r1 * cos(v)] * cos(u)
    y = [1 - r1 * cos(v)] * sin(u)
    z = r2 * [sin(v) + period_length * u / pi]
Where,
r1 = radius of the thickness of the coil
r2 = radius of the coil
u = number of points the thickness of the coil is divided into
v = number of points the radius of the coil is divided into
